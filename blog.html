DUTCH


I've never been a great writer. Last year I started to learned Dutch. This year I'm learning how to write. I can't help but commit to learning new things always between important and useless. It's the area where I can say I've achieved something but hardly apply it to the real world. Still. Won't ever be fluent. Will never be a web developer. Will never be a writer. Doing everything is fitting. Jack of all trades. I'm learning Dutch and it's close to English. So close to complete unknown, confusion. Goldilocks. This month I promised myself that I'll study the whole dictionary. The Dutch dictionary. I'll have to start on A. A has hundreds of words. Or thousands. I didn't count. I won't count. I'll go as I go. A will be the worst part. Each day I'll test myself and then go to the next letter. J, Q, Y, X, and Z won't take too long. Just like English. Three weeks. There's a very small purpose to all this. Anything less is worthless. Anything more is work. How much time and effort define those bounds? I don't know.3/10/22
