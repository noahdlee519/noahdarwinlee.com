DUTCH

I've never been a great writer. Last year I started to learned Dutch. This year I'm learning how to write. Another thing that's between important and useless. Still. Won't ever be fluent. Doing everything is fitting. Jack of all trades. I'm learning Dutch and it's close to English. So close to complete unknown, confusion. Goldilocks. This month I promised myself that I'll study the whole dictionary. The Dutch dictionary. I'll have to start on A. A has hundreds of words. Or thousands. I didn't count. I won't count. I'll go as I go. A will be the worst part. Each day I'll test myself and then go to the next letter. J, Q, Y, X, and Z won't take too long. Just like English. Three weeks. There's a very small purpose to all this. Anything less is worthless. Anything more is work. How much time and effort define those bounds?

THE LETTER E

These two projects, the website and the Dutch dictionary, are incomplete. 5 letters out of 26. But what of the website? There's progress. It's slow. E has been my favorite so far. Eenzaam is lonely. Evenwijdig means parallel. Dutch is great because the words combine in ways that almost make sense. Lonely? One-some. Parallel? Even-wide. Eergisteren means the day before yesterday. English doesn't have one word like it. The day before yesterday. Two days ago. Many words for one. Today I shortened code on the website. An easier way to scroll, mobile optimizations, font scaling. It's better. When is it best?
